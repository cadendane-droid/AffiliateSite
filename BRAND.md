# Ounce Ledger — Brand Definition

**Name:** Ounce Ledger
**Domain:** ounceledger.com (GitHub Pages URL until DNS is configured)
**Tagline:** Backpacking gear, weighed and verified.

## One-sentence promise

Every piece of backpacking gear on this site is evaluated by the numbers — verified weight, packed size, and durability evidence — so you can build a lighter pack without gambling on marketing copy.

## What we cover — and what we never cover

Ounce Ledger is **exclusively about backpacking gear**: gear you carry on your back, on foot, into the backcountry. Weight, packability, and trail durability are the lens for every evaluation.

Out of scope, permanently: car camping, overlanding, RV gear, tailgating, general "outdoor lifestyle" products, home goods with an outdoor theme. If it only makes sense within 100 feet of a vehicle, it does not belong here.

All content belongs to exactly one of these nine categories:

`shelters` · `sleep-systems` · `backpacks` · `cooking` · `water` · `clothing` · `footwear` · `navigation-safety` · `tools-repair`

## Target reader

Thru-hikers and multi-day backpackers, beginner to intermediate. They are planning a real trip — a weekend loop, a section hike, or a full thru-hike — and they are trying to spend money once, correctly. They can handle numbers. They distrust hype. They will notice if a claimed weight is wrong.

## Editorial voice

Practical. Weight-obsessed. Skeptical of marketing claims. Plainspoken.

Rules:

1. **Lead with the number.** Weight in ounces *and* grams, always. Packed size when it matters. If the manufacturer doesn't publish a spec, say "not published" — never estimate.
2. **Attribute every claim.** We synthesize manufacturer specs, verified owner reviews, and published third-party testing. We say so. We never imply first-hand field testing that didn't happen.
3. **Name the trade-off.** No product is recommended without stating what you give up. A 1.9 oz stove that's unstable in wind is described exactly that way.
4. **Price in bands.** `$` / `$$` / `$$$` in body copy. Exact dollar figures go stale and are banned outside the affiliate registry.
5. **Short sentences. Active voice.** Write like a trail-mate explaining gear at a shakedown, not a catalog.
6. **Skepticism is the default.** Marketing terms ("military-grade," "aerospace fabric") are either translated into a verifiable spec or flagged as unverifiable.

## Banned words and phrases

Enforced by `scripts/integrity-check.mjs` — the build fails if these appear in content:

- **Hype adjectives:** game-changer, game changing, must-have, revolutionary, ultimate, epic, insane, unbeatable, best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge, next-level, top-notch
- **Fabricated experience:** "we tested," "our testing," "in our testing," "I used," "we used it," "after N miles," "we wore," "we carried," "our testers," "hands-on review"
- **Fake urgency/scarcity:** "limited time," "act now," "selling fast," "only N left," "while supplies last," "don't miss out"

## Visual identity

Ledger aesthetic: off-white paper background, near-black ink, a single rust-orange accent, tabular figures for every spec. Dense, quiet, trustworthy — closer to a well-kept logbook than a lifestyle magazine. System font stack only (performance budget: Lighthouse ≥ 95).
